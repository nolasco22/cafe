Coperativa Kafe Timor About Background

About Background Cooperativa Café Timor (CCT) is the Timor Leste’s largest agriculture cooperative with 28,000 members. It produces high grade coffee, spices and cassava flour for domestic and export sale. CCT is owned, managed and staffed by East Timorese, operating in 11 of 13 Municipalities of the country. For the last 17 years, CCT has operated “free of charge” primary health care services for the benefit of rural communities in the coffee growing regions of Ermera, Ainaro, Aileu, Liquica and Manufhai Municipalities. Health services include general medical, dental and maternal and child health services. CCT utilizes diverse but integrated models of service delivery tailored to meet the unique health and social development needs of rural/ remote Timorese’s families. CCT has fixed and mobile clinical services enhanced with outreach village based services. CCT’s Men’s Health Program is a unique community based volunteer program addressing issues of men in health; nutrition; gender issues; social inclusion and income management. CCT’s health activities are sustained by the sale of the cooperative’s agricultural produce and works closely with the Timor Leste Ministry of Health to improve the health status of rural Timorese.
